## 1.7.3 Agile Methods  

- **Agile** develops systems **incrementally** via **prototypes**, adjusting constantly to **user feedback**.  
- Inspired by **Japanese auto firms** using **team-based flexible manufacturing**.  
- Uses the **spiral model**: repeated **Planning â†’ Risk Analysis â†’ Engineering â†’ Evaluation**.  
- Each loop ends with user **accept/reject/change** decisions.  

**Pros:**  
- Continuous **feedback**, reduces **risks**, improves **speed**.  

**Cons:**  
- Weak **documentation**, blurred **accountability**, risk of drifting from **business strategy**, cost creep with too many iterations.  

**Popular methods:** **Scrum**, **Extreme Programming (XP)**, **Kanban**, **Lean**.  

> ðŸ’¡ *Example:* Building a **clinic appointment app** â†’ deliver login screen week 1, add scheduling week 2, reminders week 3â€”**working software every step**.

## 1.7.4 Prototyping  

- **Prototype** = early working model of a system.  
- Validates **inputs**, **outputs**, and **interfaces** before final build.  
- Can be:  
  - **Throwaway:** learn and discard.  
  - **Evolutionary:** prototype grows into final system.  

**Pros:** Speeds learning, reduces risk.  
**Cons:** Can **lock bad decisions early** or carry **technical debt** forward.  

> ðŸ’¡ *Example:* Build a **wireframe** of a patient intake form, test with staff, refine before coding the database.

## 1.7.5 Tools  

- **ALM / PLM (Application/Product Lifecycle Management):** manages entire system lifecycle. Example: **Jira**.  
- **IDE (Integrated Development Environment):** coding/debugging environment. Example: **Visual Studio**, **VS Code**.  
- **CASE (Computer-Aided Software Engineering):** modeling + sometimes code generation. Example: **Cameo Systems Modeler**.  

**Why tools matter:** Increase **productivity**, ensure **quality**, enforce **standards**.

## 1.9 The Systems Analyst  

### 1.9.1 Role  
- **Investigate, analyze, design, develop, evaluate, maintain** systems.  
- Acts as a **translator** between **business needs** and **technical implementation**.  
- Builds **models, diagrams, decision tables**.  

### 1.9.2 Knowledge, Skills, and Education  

- **Technical Knowledge:** Stay current (INCOSE, training, communities).  
- **Communication Skills:** Clear writing, presentations, team coordination.  
- **Business Skills:** Understand operations, finance, strategy.  
- **Critical Thinking:** Compare, evaluate, recognize patterns, logic.  
- **Education:** Typically **IS, CS, or Business degree**; experience or **certs** can substitute.  

### 1.9.3 Certification  

- Validates knowledge/skills (e.g., **cloud, database, security, project management**).  
- Not a guarantee of competence but **essential for career growth**.  

### 1.9.4 Career Opportunities  

- Strong demand (esp. **e-commerce**, **digital transformation**).  
- Paths in **small firms** (broad roles), **large firms** (specialization), **consulting**.  
- Job titles may vary: **Systems Analyst, Software Engineer, Programmer Analyst**, etc.  

**What to vet:** org structure, size, salary, culture, growth.  

### 1.9.5 Trends in Information Technology  

- **Agile methods**: now mainstream.  
- **Cloud computing**: scalable, pay-as-you-go resources.  
- **Data science/AI/ML**: predictive analytics, big data.  
- **Mobile/Edge**: smartphones, BYOD, edge compute.  
- **Service Orientation**: mix of products + services.  
- **Social Media**: massive user reach, integration with IT trends.  

> ðŸ’¡ *Example:* A retailer shifts **inventory** to **cloud**, adds **AI demand forecasting**, launches a **mobile app**, and markets via **social media ads**â€”tying multiple trends together.

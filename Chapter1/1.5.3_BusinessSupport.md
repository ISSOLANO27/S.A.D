
# Notes: Business Support Systems

## What are Business Support Systems?

* **Business support systems** provide **job-related information support** to users at all levels of a company.
* Functions:

  * Analyze transactional data
  * Generate information for managing and controlling business processes
  * Provide insights for better decision-making

---

## History

* Early b
siness computers replaced manual tasks (e.g., payroll processing).
* Over time, companies realized computers could also **produce valuable information**.
* These evolved into **Management Information Systems (MIS)**, initially aimed at managers.
* Today: **Employees at all levels** rely on these systems for their jobs.

---

## Relationship with TP Systems

* Business support systems often work **hand-in-hand with transaction processing systems**.

  * Example: A TP system records a sale, updates balance, deducts inventory.
  * The business support system highlights trends:

    * Fast- or slow-moving items
    * Past-due balances
    * Inventory needing adjustment

---

## Data Collection & RFID

* Firms must collect **production, sales, and shipping data** to compete effectively.
* **Automated data acquisition** uses **RFID (Radio Frequency Identification)**.

  * Uses high-frequency radio waves to track objects (e.g., shirt in *Figure 1-12*).
  * Retailers like Walmart require suppliers to tag all items.
  * Allows **real-time monitoring** of goods through the supply chain.

---

## Decision Support

* **Decision support capability** = key feature of business support systems.
* Helps users make decisions by running **computer models with variables**.
* Examples:

  * Truck fleet dispatcher runs *what-if* scenarios for weather or shipment increases.
  * Retailer uses *what-if* analysis to set prices for maximizing profit while holding volume and costs constant.

---

## Key Takeaways

* Business support systems go **beyond transactions** → they add **analysis and insight**.
* MIS → broader view of company operations.
* RFID → automation and visibility in logistics and retail.
* Decision support = enables forecasting and strategy through *what-if* scenarios.

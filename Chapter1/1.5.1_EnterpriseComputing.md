
# Notes: Enterprise Computing

## What is Enterprise Computing?

* **Enterprise computing** = information systems that support company-wide operations and data management requirements.
* Examples:

  * Walmart’s inventory control system
  * Boeing’s production control system
  * Hilton Hotels’ reservation system

---

## Objectives

* Integrate a company’s **primary functions**:

  * Production
  * Sales
  * Services
  * Inventory control
  * Accounting
* Benefits:

  * Improves efficiency
  * Reduces costs
  * Helps managers make key decisions
  * Enhances **data security and reliability** via centralized frameworks

---

## Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP)

* **ERP systems** = applications that provide **cost-effective support** for users and managers throughout a company.
* Example: A car rental company uses ERP to **forecast customer demand** at hundreds of locations.
* Growth: Many vendors (hardware & software) target ERP because of its widespread adoption and potential.

---

## Advantages of Enterprise Computing

* Dramatic **cost reductions** through a unified, company-wide computing environment.
* Better decision-making support.
* Company-wide **integration of data** and processes.

---

## Disadvantages of Enterprise Computing

* **High implementation cost** in time, money, and commitment.
* ERP systems often impose a **rigid structure** that might not fit a company’s unique operations.
